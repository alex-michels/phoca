PHOCA

The seal coin, 2,600 years in the making

The first seal coin was minted 2,600 years ago

Phṓkē (φώκη) is the ancient Greek word for seal. Phoca is the Latin name science still uses for the genus of true seals — Phoca vitulina, the harbor seal, lives along Europe's North Sea and Baltic coasts today.

The Greek city of Phocaea — "seal city," on the Aegean coast of today's Türkiye — was among the first places in the world to mint coins, around 600 BC. Its coins were stamped with the city's badge: a seal. A Phocaean coin from 600–550 BC sits in the British Museum. The Phocaeans were legendary sailors — they founded Marseille around 600 BC, and Herodotus credits them with pioneering the long sea voyages of their age.

Some of Phocaea's coins depict what today is identified with the Mediterranean monk seal — now one of the most endangered marine mammals on Earth. The animal that once symbolized a city's money now needs help surviving. That is the gap PHOCA exists to help close.

What PHOCA is

PHOCA is a community token project on Solana for seal lovers worldwide — and a funding mechanism for seal rescue and marine mammal protection that does not depend on anyone's promise:

  • PHOCA uses Solana's Token-2022 standard with a built-in transfer fee. On every transfer, the blockchain itself withholds a small percentage — this rule is enforced by the token program, not by the team.
  • Withheld fees can only be collected by the designated treasury authority, and every collection is a public, permanently visible transaction — see the live transparency log, written automatically by the collection script itself.
  • Our commitment: fee proceeds fund seal rescue centers, marine mammal research and ocean protection, with every donation published as an on-chain transaction link plus the organization's confirmation. We name partner organizations only with their written agreement.

What PHOCA is not

  • PHOCA is not an investment. It grants no claim to profit, dividends, revenue, or the charity funds. Nobody — including us — should acquire PHOCA expecting financial return.
