# 🦭 Charity policy — who the fees help, and how we decide

**Status: working policy for the devnet phase.** It becomes a binding public
commitment at launch; final legal review is a Phase 4 gate
(docs/COMPLIANCE-EU.md). Wording rules of Phase C apply to this document.

## The focus: pinnipeds

PHOCA's charity fee exists for **pinnipeds** — true seals, sea lions and fur
seals — with rescue as the first priority. Special attention goes to the
species and colonies where sheer numbers make the need greatest: the large
fur seal and sea lion populations, where a single entanglement season can
mean thousands of animals in trouble.

Priority order:
1. **Rescue & rehabilitation** — centers that take in injured, orphaned or
   entangled animals, in the EU and worldwide.
2. **Direct-intervention projects** — disentanglement and debris-rescue work
   (for example, freeing fur seals from fishing line and plastic waste off
   the coast of Namibia — the kind of work where one small team saves
   hundreds of animals a year).
3. **Research & monitoring** of pinniped populations and their health.
4. **Habitat and ocean protection** with a direct, explainable benefit to
   pinnipeds.

## Deliberately NOT a fixed list of organizations

We do not maintain a named list of beneficiary organizations, on purpose:

- **Needs are spontaneous.** This month it may be a debris-entanglement
  emergency in Namibia; next month, a rehabilitation center in the EU with
  a pup season overload; after that, a monk-seal project in the
  Mediterranean. A fixed list would tie the fee to yesterday's needs.
- **Naming creates false expectations** — both for holders ("why did X get
  less this month?") and for organizations, who never agreed to be part of
  a crypto project's marketing. Our standing rule (docs/TOKENOMICS.md):
  **no organization is ever named as a partner without its written
  agreement.**
- **Flexibility is not vagueness** — every single donation is still fully
  accountable, per the rules below.

## How a recipient is chosen (all must hold)

1. **Real and verifiable** — a registered organization or a publicly
   documented project with an observable track record. No anonymous
   recipients, no individuals without a verifiable project.
2. **Direct pinniped impact** — the money must map to rescue, care,
   research or protection of seals, sea lions or fur seals; "ocean-adjacent"
   is not enough.
3. **Able to receive funds cleanly** — crypto directly, via a donation
   intermediary, or after conversion to fiat; whichever path, it must be
   traceable end to end.
4. **Willing to have the donation confirmed** — publicly, or at minimum in
   writing to us for the transparency report.
5. **No conflicts of interest** — nothing flows to the team, relatives, or
   any entity the team controls. Ever.

## Accountability (what makes this trustworthy)

Every donation, no exceptions:
- the on-chain transaction link, published in the transparency report
  (docs/TRANSPARENCY.md, fed by docs/TRANSPARENCY-LOG.md);
- a short written rationale: which category above, why this recipient, why
  this amount;
- the recipient's confirmation once received.

Charity claims in ANY public material may only describe what is actually
implemented on-chain and actually donated (CLAUDE.md rule 7). An unfulfilled
charity promise is not just a compliance problem — it is the one thing this
project must never do.
