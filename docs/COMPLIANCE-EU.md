# 🇪🇺 EU / MiCA compliance workstream (living document — NOT legal advice)

PHOCA under MiCA is a "crypto-asset other than an ART or EMT" (Title II) —
not a stablecoin, so no issuer license is needed, but real obligations apply
the moment you offer the token to the EU public or seek a trading listing.
Everything below must be validated by a crypto-savvy lawyer (Germany: one who
works with BaFin filings) BEFORE any public offer. Treat this as your agenda
for that meeting, not as the answer.

## Phase A — before ANY public communication about buying PHOCA
- [ ] Decide the offer structure. Check with counsel whether an exemption fits:
      genuinely free airdrops (no payment or data exchanged), offers to fewer
      than 150 persons per member state, offers below the small-offer threshold
      (~€1M over 12 months — confirm exact current figure), or qualified
      investors only. If exempt, document WHY in writing.
- [ ] Form a legal entity (e.g. German UG/GmbH). Anonymous public fundraising
      is not compatible with MiCA. Entity holds trademarks, domains, treasury.
- [ ] Open the trademark/naming file: EUIPO search for PHOCA in relevant
      classes, register logo + word/figurative combination.

## Phase B — the white paper (if no exemption applies)
- [ ] Draft using the EU's mandated template and content set: issuer identity,
      project description, rights & obligations attached to the token
      (spell out that PHOCA grants NO claim to profit, dividends, or the
      charity funds), underlying technology, risks, and the mandatory
      sustainability/energy disclosures (Solana is proof-of-stake and
      low-energy — good position, but the disclosure still uses ESMA's
      methodology, not our own words).
- [ ] Produce it in the required machine-readable format (XHTML with inline
      XBRL tagging per ESMA's technical standards) — this is a technical
      deliverable, budget for it.
- [ ] Notify/lodge with BaFin within the statutory timeline before publication
      (MiCA works on notification for Title II tokens, not pre-approval — but
      the regulator reads and can push back).
- [ ] Publish on the official website before the offer starts; add the
      required summary; keep every version archived.
- [ ] Understand issuer liability: incomplete, unclear or misleading white
      paper = liability to token holders for losses. This is why every claim
      in it (including the Phocaea history and the charity mechanics) must be
      precise and verifiable.
- [ ] Ask counsel about the retail right of withdrawal for purchases made
      directly from the issuer during an offer (a ~14-day cooling-off right
      exists — build refund mechanics before, not after).

## Phase C — marketing & communications discipline (applies ALWAYS)
- [ ] All marketing must be fair, clear, not misleading, consistent with the
      white paper, and carry risk warnings. This includes memes. Yes, memes.
- [ ] Paid influencer promotion = marketing communication under MiCA, same
      rules + disclosure of the paid relationship.
- [ ] Never promise or imply profit ("100x", "to the moon" from official
      channels = trouble). Community members can meme; the ISSUER cannot.
- [ ] Charity claims: name partner organizations only with written agreements;
      publish the exact fee-split; every donation gets an on-chain tx link and
      a confirmation (see docs/TRANSPARENCY.md). An unfulfilled charity claim
      is not just a MiCA problem — it's consumer-protection fraud territory.

## Phase D — what we are NOT doing (scope fences)
- We do NOT custody user funds, run an exchange, execute orders, or advise —
  those are CASP activities needing authorization. If that ever changes,
  stop and get counsel first.
- We do NOT issue anything price-pegged (that would be ART/EMT territory —
  a completely different, licensed regime).

## Phase E — ongoing after launch
- [ ] Material changes to the project → assess white paper update duty.
- [ ] Keep the transparency cadence (monthly report) — it doubles as evidence
      of fair communication.
- [ ] German tax: token sales, collected fees, and donations all have
      consequences — retain a crypto-experienced Steuerberater from day one.

Budget line to accept early: proper MiCA legal support costs real money.
It is cheaper than one enforcement letter. Devnet learning needs none of this.
