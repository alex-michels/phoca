## The name

*Phṓkē* (φώκη) is the ancient Greek word for seal. *Phoca* is the Latin name
science still uses for the genus of true seals — *Phoca vitulina*, the harbor
seal, lives along Europe's North Sea and Baltic coasts today.

## The first seal coin was minted 2,600 years ago

The Greek city of **Phocaea** — "seal city," on the Aegean coast of today's
Türkiye — was among the first places in the world to mint coins, around
600 BC. Its coins were stamped with the city's badge: **a seal**. A Phocaean
coin from 600–550 BC sits in the British Museum. The Phocaeans were legendary
sailors — they founded Marseille around 600 BC, and Herodotus credits them
with pioneering the long sea voyages of their age.

Some of Phocaea's coins depict what today is identified with the
Mediterranean monk seal — now one of the most endangered marine mammals on
Earth. The animal that once symbolized a city's money now needs help
surviving. That is the gap PHOCA exists to help close.

**The first seal coin was minted 2,600 years ago. PHOCA is the next one —
this time, for the seals themselves.**

## What PHOCA is

PHOCA is a community token on Solana for seal lovers worldwide — and a
funding mechanism for seal rescue and marine mammal protection that does not
depend on anyone's promise:

- PHOCA uses Solana's Token-2022 standard with a built-in **transfer fee**.
  On every transfer, the blockchain itself withholds a small percentage —
  this rule is enforced by the token program, not by the team.
- Withheld fees can only be collected by the designated treasury authority,
  and every collection is a public, permanently visible transaction.
- Our commitment: fee proceeds fund seal rescue centers, marine mammal
  research and ocean protection, with **every donation published as an
  on-chain transaction link plus the organization's confirmation** in a
  regular transparency report. We name partner organizations only with
  their written agreement.

## What PHOCA is not

- PHOCA is **not an investment**. It grants no claim to profit, dividends,
  revenue, or the charity funds. Nobody — including us — should buy PHOCA
  expecting financial return.
- Crypto-assets are volatile and can lose all value. Never spend money you
  cannot afford to lose.
- PHOCA is not a stablecoin and is not pegged to anything.

## Where the project stands

PHOCA currently runs on Solana's **devnet** (test network) as a learning and
engineering project. Nothing is deployed to mainnet, nothing is for sale, and
nothing here is an offer. A public launch happens only after our published
launch gates are met: independent-of-us fee enforcement verified, authorities
locked down or revoked, liquidity locked with public proof, and the EU legal
groundwork (MiCA) completed with counsel. The full plan is public in this
repository — the roadmap *is* the marketing.
