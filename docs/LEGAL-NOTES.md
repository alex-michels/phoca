## 2. EU / Germany: MiCA applies to you
The EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) has fully applied since Dec 30, 2024.
For "other crypto-assets" (which a meme/utility token typically is), offering a token to
the EU public generally requires, among other things:
- acting through a **legal entity** (no anonymous public fundraising),
- a **crypto-asset white paper** in the EU's mandated template/format, notified to the
  national regulator (in Germany: BaFin) and published before the offer,
- fair, clear, non-misleading marketing with risk warnings.
There are narrow exemptions (e.g. genuinely free airdrops, small/limited offers) — whether
you fit one is exactly the question for a crypto-savvy lawyer BEFORE launch, not after.

## 3. Charity claims = extra duty of honesty
If you advertise "part of fees goes to seal rescue", that promise is legally meaningful.
Misusing it can be fraud / misleading commercial practice. Also check German rules around
soliciting donations, and whether the charity actually accepts crypto.

## 4. Taxes
Token sales, fees you collect, and even donations have tax consequences in Germany.
Get a Steuerberater who has seen crypto before.
